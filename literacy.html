Computer Literacy

What are Bridging the Digital Divide Computer Literacy Classes?

Each semester, the Bridging the Digital Divide Program (BDDP) recruits student volunteers to provide basic computer literacy instruction to community members. Groups of instructors teach at off-campus locations and to a range of audiences. Extensive computing knowledge is NOT necessary to teach these basic skill-level courses. Instructors must be patient, compassionate individuals who wish to help others and are not afraid to command a classroom.

Teaching Computer Literacy

Instructors can expect to teach 2-4 per week. Classes typically occur twice a week, and last for 3-6 weeks. The length of instruction time varies depending on the needs of the audience and partnering organizations. Instructors are provided with a tested, pre-developed curriculum, exercise files, and handout files to aid in their teaching.

Population types that typically participate in computer literacy classes are youth, immigrants & refugees, unemployed and underemployed adults, senior citizens, retired adults, formerly homeless adults, and people with mental illness.

Opportunities for student instructors include:

  • For-credit Independent studies*
  • Credit and Non-credit Internships*
  • Voluntary Service
  • Service-Learning Credit (SUNY Broome only)

*Require departmental approval

Teaching is structured in several ways:


Structured Classes

Community members sign up ahead of time and follow a curriculum for a predetermined amount of time

Drop-in Hours

Volunteers work set hours each week and community members drop-in with questions

Small Group Tutoring

Volunteers work with one, two, or three community members to address their individual needs

Class Materials

Each class focuses on one of three levels:

Level 1 (Discoverer)

  • Computer Basics
  • Windows Basics
  • File Management
  • Word Processing
  • Elective Topics

Level 2 (Explorer)

  • Microsoft Word
  • Internet Basics
  • Email
  • Social Media
  • Instant Messaging
  • Elective Topics

Level 3 (Navigator)

  • Advanced Microsoft Word
  • Microsoft Powerpoint
  • PC Performance
  • The Internet and Academic Research
  • Advanced Google Search Tips

Instructors are provided with a variety of materials in order to help them achieve their maximum teaching potential, including:

  • A manual that acts as a comprehensive step-by-step guide for instructors
  • Topic descriptions that provide a quick overview of each topic
  • Exercise files that provide community members hands-on practice
  • Hand-out files that instructors may use to supplement instruction

Partners and Clients

Each semester, BDDP partners with organizations and agencies in the local community. These organizations are responsible for 1) recruiting community members who are interested in receiving computer literacy instruction; 2) providing a location for classes to be held. Get Involved!

Only Binghamton University and SUNY Broome students may apply to teach computer literacy instruction through BDDP. Once classes begin (early to mid-semester), BDDP usually does not accept more volunteers. To avoid missing the application deadline, it is encouraged that you apply at the start of the semester in which you wish to volunteer or a semester in advance. Binghamton University students should fill out the application form and send it to BDDP@binghamton.edu SUNY Broome students should email wrightsk@sunybroome.edu to indicate their interest.
