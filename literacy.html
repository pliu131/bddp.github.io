Computer Literacy

What are Bridging the Digital Divide Computer Literacy Classes?

Each semester, the Bridging the Digital Divide Program (BDDP) recruits student volunteers to provide basic computer literacy instruction to community members. Instructors are partnered with 1-2 other students, and teach at off-campus locations each week. Basic knowledge and facility with Windows and Microsoft Office, patience, commitment and enthusiasm are all that is required in order to be a computer literacy Instructor for BDDP. Previous teaching experience is a plus. In addition, individuals must be comfortable speaking in front of groups and working with diverse populations.

Teaching Computer Literacy

Instructors can expect to allot 2-4 hours per week to teaching. Classes typically occur twice a week, and last for 3-6 to weeks. The length of instruction varies depending on the needs of the clients and partnering organizations.

Population types that typically participate in computer literacy classes are youth, immigrants & refugees, unemployed and underemployed adults, senior citizens, retired adults, formerly homeless adults, and people with mental illness.

Opportunities for student instructors include:

  • For-credit Independent studies*
  • Internship credit & non-credit*
  • Participation on a solely volunteer basis
  • Participation as a group or pair

Note: Most students who participate in this program as computer literacy instructors do so solely as volunteers (i.e. not for-credit).
*Require departmental approval

Teaching is structured in several ways:


Structured Classes

Community members sign up ahead of time and follow a curriculum for a predetermined amount of time

Drop-in Hours

Volunteers work set hours each week and community members drop-in with questions

Small Group Tutoring

Volunteers work with one, two, or three community members to address their individual needs

Class Materials

Each class focuses on one of three levels:

Level 1 (Discoverer)

  • Computer Basics
  • Windows Basics
  • File Management
  • Word Processing
  • Elective Topics

Level 2 (Explorer)

  • Microsoft Word
  • Internet Basics
  • Email
  • Social Media
  • Instant Messaging
  • Elective Topics

Level 3 (Navigator)

  • Advanced Microsoft Word
  • Microsoft Powerpoint
  • PC Performance
  • The Internet and Academic Research
  • Advanced Google Search Tips

Instructors are provided with a variety of materials in order to help them achieve their maximum teaching potential, including:

  • A manual that acts as a comprehensive step-by-step guide for instructors
  • Topic descriptions that provide a quick overview of each topic
  • Exercise files that provide community members hands-on practice
  • Hand-out files that instructors may use to supplement instruction

Partners and Clients

Each semester, BDDP partners with organizations in the local community. These organizations are responsible for recruiting community members to receive computer literacy instruction, as well as providing a location for classes to be taught.


Get Involved!

Please complete the volunteer application and send it to bddp@binghamton.edu to apply to be a computer literacy instructor. Once classes begin (mid-semester), BDDP usually does not accept more volunteers. Thus, if you do not receive an immediate reply, it may be due to the fact that classes have already started and the program has finished recruiting for the semester. It is best to apply at the start of the semester or a semester in advance of when you are interested in getting involved.
